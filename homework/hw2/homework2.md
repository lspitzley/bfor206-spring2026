# Homework 2: Requirements Gathering

## 1. Product Owner Interviews — 2 points

- **1 point** — Interview #1 conducted and documented  
- **1 point** — Interview #2 conducted and documented  

Interviews must include meaningful elicitation (questions + captured responses).

---

## 2. Team Sub‑Area Designation — 1 point

Once you have a sense of the different components of your area, divide these into sub‑areas and assign them to team members. Each sub‑area should be reasonably sized and focused on a specific aspect of the project. For example, if you are working on the questions,
a sub-area might be defining the types of
questions.

- **1 point** — Clear identification of which team member is responsible for which subsystem / feature area

---

## 3. Sub‑Area Requirements Evaluation

Each team member’s designated sub‑area will be graded on the following criteria:

### a. Matches Product Owner Needs — 1 point

- Requirements accurately reflect what the product owner requested.

### b. Consistency — 1 point

- Requirements do not contradict one another.  
- Terms, conditions, and assumptions align across the sub‑area.

### c. Unambiguous — 1 point

- Requirements avoid vague language such as:
  *“fast,” “user-friendly,” “optimize,” “intuitive,” etc.*  
- Each requirement has only one reasonable interpretation.

### d. Complete — 1 point

- All reasonable cases are defined.  
- No placeholders such as “TBD”.

### e. Feasible — 1 point

- Each requirement is realistically implementable given technology and time constraints.

### f. Verifiable — 1 point

- There must be a clear and objective way to test whether the requirement is met.

---

## 4. Version Control Activity — 2 points

- **1 point** — At least *two commits* per team member  
- **1 point** — Commits are spread across **48+ hours** (not all at once)

---

- **2 points** — A Pull Request (PR) for the requirements document is opened and accepted by the instructor.
